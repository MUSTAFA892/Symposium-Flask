IPL Auction:

In the IPL Auction event, participants simulate a cricket player auction, managing a budget to bid for players. The goal is to build the most competitive team within budget constraints. Success is judged on strategic player selection and team composition.


Reel Making Competition:

In the Reel Making Competition, participants create engaging reels based on an on-the-spot topic. Reels must be 60 seconds or less, with original content and optional music or sound effects. Creativity and adherence to the topic are key to winning.


Photography:

In the Photography Event, participants capture images based on a specific theme or prompt. Judging focuses on creativity, composition, and adherence to the theme. The most compelling and well-executed photographs will be awarded.


Chess:

In the Chess Competition, participants compete in a Swiss system format with multiple rounds. Matches follow standard chess rules, and scoring is based on wins, draws, and losses. Strategy, skill, and time management are crucial for success.


E-sports:

In the eSports Competition, participants compete in a series of gaming matches across various titles. Success is determined by strategic play, skill, and teamwork. Points are awarded based on match outcomes, with top performers advancing through the rounds.

IPL Auction Rules:


  • Team Composition:
    • Maximum of 3 members per team.
  • Tournament Structure:
    • 2 Rounds in total.
  • Round 1: Quiz
    • A quiz on IPL with 15 questions.
    • Teams' scores and timing will be recorded.
    • Top 10 teams with the highest scores and best timing advance to Round 2.
  • Round 2: Auction
    • Auction Purse: 100 crores.
    • 5 Categories of players:
      • Batsmen
      • Bowlers
      • All-Rounders
      • Uncapped Players
    • Teams choose a franchise name from IPL.
    • Players have a base price and a rating.
    • Each team must select 15 players (maximum of 4 foreign players).
    • Teams select their playing XI from the 15 players based on player ratings.
  • Winner Determination:
    • The XI with the highest rating wins.
    • In case of a tie in ratings, the team with the highest remaining purse wins.

Reel Making Competition Rules:


  • Team Size: 1-3 members
  • Reel Length: Must be 60 seconds or less
  • Total Rounds: 1
  • Topic: Will be given on the spot
  • Music or Sound Effects: Allowed
  • Content: Only original content is allowed. No copyrighted material may be used.

Photography Rules:


  • Participant Type: Individual participants only.
  • Event Structure:
    • Total of 2 Rounds.
    • Different topic will be given in each round.
    • Both mobile and camera are allowed.
    • Images should be submitted within the given time.
    • Photos must be taken within the Kite Campus surroundings.
    • Images must relate to the given topic.
    • Submit images to the drive shared by the coordinator.
  • Round 1:
    • Topic will be given on the spot.
    • Submit 2-3 images for this round.
    • Duration: 20 minutes.
    • Top 10 best images will move to Round 2.
  • Round 2:
    • New topic will be given for this round.
    • Each participant can submit only 2 images.
    • Extra points for describing the pictures.
    • Duration: 20 minutes.

Chess Tournament Rules:


  • Event Setup:
    • Format: Swiss system with multiple rounds (5 or 6 rounds) without elimination.
    • Date and Venue: To be chosen on campus.
    • Basic Rules: Standard chess rules apply (e.g., checkmate, draw conditions, touch-move).
  • Registration:
    • Sign-Up: Create a registration form with a deadline for students to register.
    • Participants: Confirm the list of players before the event.
  • Scoring:
    • Win: 1 point
    • Draw: 0.5 points
    • Loss: 0 points
  • Time Control:
    • Time: 15 minutes per player, plus 5 minutes increment per move.
  • Additional Rules and Regulations:
    • Players must adhere to the touch-move rule.
    • Players must be on time for each round; late arrivals may be penalized.
    • All decisions by the tournament organizers and arbiters are final and binding.

E-Sports Rules:


  • BGMI / Free Fire:
    • 4-man squad preferred.
    • No ESP/Emulators allowed.
    • Accessories like triggers, finger sleeves, coolers are allowed.
    • Ensure maximum charge; venue may not have charging ports.
    • Manage internet connectivity issues yourself.
    • Format: 2 Battle Royale games, 1 TDM (If two teams finish at the same place).
  • eFootball:
    • Ensure maximum charge; venue may not have charging ports.
    • Format: 1v1 matches, Knockout Stages.
